The ingredient selection menu can be used to select an ingredient for a crafting recipe, container recipe, or the repair item of a custom tool (the repair item of a tool is technically very similar to the ingredient of a recipe, so the same menu can be used for both). It should look like this:

Note: if you want a custom amount or remaining item, you need to select it before clicking on Custom Item, Simple vanilla item, or Vanilla item with datavalue.

  • Clicking the Cancel button will take you back to the menu you came from, without adding or replacing the ingredient.
  • The Amount field can be used to let the ingredient require a stacksize that is larger than 1, and decrease that stacksize by more than 1. This makes it possible to add crafting recipes that require for instance 10 iron ingots (which would normally be impossible because a crafting table only has 9 slots).
  • The Remaining item can be used to replace the ingredient with another item rather than removing it upon crafting. For instance, you can choose a bucket as the remaining item of a lava bucket ingredient. When the result item is crafted, the lava bucket will be replaced with an empty bucket rather than disappearing entirely.
  • Clicking Custom Item will take you to the custom item selection menu, where you can choose a custom item as the ingredient.
  • Clicking Simple vanilla item will take you to the simple vanilla item selection menu, where you can choose a simple vanilla item as ingredient (a standard minecraft item like a stick).
  • Clicking Vanilla item with datavalue will take you to the vanilla item with data selection menu, where you can choose a vanilla item plus a data value as ingredient. Note: this data value is just an integer between 0 and 16 used before minecraft 1.13 to distinguish things like the different colors of wool.
